The Mental Self

Sometimes, it is inspiring to believe that "mind is over matter." It is rather a powerful motivation and it really is true often times. However, I believe this has some limits too but I can't quite define the line that separate it. It may be dependent on physical health or even emotional health, etc. Anyways, I will be discussing the various aspects of my mental self.

Cognition and Intelligence

Assessing my cognition and intelligence, on other people's perspective many people around me think I have better than average cognition, decision-making, logic, and even math skills. However, for me, I digress from that statement since I think I am only able to have all these due to my privilege, circumstance, and even luck. However, due to all these, I use this opportunity to make it useful by applying what I have learned and experienced to make a difference.

My logic is also often times hasty and I miss out on some things. Thus I tend to be careless and my problem-solving skills tend to get negatively affected by this. However, when it all works out, my brain then has Eureka! moments that help me wittily solve problems in very simple ways.

Memory

The most terrible aspect of my Mental Self, is my memory. This used to be my niche prior to elementary where I used to have near perfect photographic memory. In just one glance at the whiteboard/blackboard, I can already jot down everything written in it to my notebook. However, through some unknown circumstance, this memory faltered and as I grew it got less reliable and even now, in solving problems, relaying information, I need to double check and verify. However, I can't always do these, therefore, I try memory exercises, studying, and getting my memory working more reliably.
