6 DIMENSIONS OF THE SELF > The Social Self

The Social Self

Humans as part of society have different roles to play and are given tasks, that are imbibed in culture, the internet, and through materials and symbolisms. As a part of this society I have been influenced by it to play a role within it as well. In this page I will be discussing what influenced me and what I am doing or have done in these aspects.

The Cultural Self

As a Filipino, we are often not a big player in the world order. That's because we are a nation made to believe so as I have believed that way back too. We've been colonized thrice for roughly three‐and‐a‐half centuries. However, as I have been exposed to many opportunities, competing against other countries and meeting awesome Filipinos along the way, we have really a big potential but sadly, our potentials aren't supported by the powers‐that‐be.

However, as we are going to be part of the nation's future our generation is pushing for more inclusivity, peace, and equity. I am hopeful for our culture as Filipinos to change for the better as our generation shows promise and hope.

Socio-Digital Self

Being born in this generation and context, having a socio-digital personality and involvement in the cyberspace is already the norm. Much more in this setting that we are now in a digital class setting.

What I can really say about myself regarding my use in the cyberspace is I am really wack in such a way that I am really particular into memes and making music parodies just like what I did in other projects.

From time to time, I also engage in my nuclear advocacies in the internet as as cyberactivism to call out the incompetence of the government due to the lives that are at stake.

Also, I use the cyberspace as a learning resource, other than it being the venue of our online classes by reading posts made by real people regarding social issues, trends, etc giving me awareness about them and internet culture.

The Material Self

Also, born into an Ilokano household, I am accustomed to being "kuripot" being a stereotype of being Ilokano. Although not to an extreme extent, being accustomed to using and buying only what is needed is actually helpful for us as it gives us a modest lifestyle but enough in times of emergencies.
