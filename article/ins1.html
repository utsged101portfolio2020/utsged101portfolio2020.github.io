Insights > Module 1

This image shows what it feels like in Module 1.

Learning about oneself, having the ability to assess myself better and knowing more about myself is what I got from the lessons in GED101 Module 1. From interpolating various historical philosophers' ideas, and theories, to reading recent studies about self and identity, got me more information about the inner and outer workings of man ad myself as well.

Despite the complexity of human psychology, there seems to be distinguishing factors of the selves and identities of one another. There are even trends that can be seen in various studies compiled in the readings. It is quite enlightening to realize something about my self and others too. Especially the fact that the nurturing years are fundamental since when it's done, it would be more difficult for one to change what was already ingrained on oneself. This gave me a clearer explanation as to the debate if one can still learn when one is older already. Just like a stack of books, it's difficult to change the book in between if there's already too many on the top. But still, you can put more on the stack of books on the top. Yes, it is possible to learn however, unlearning stuff that's already been ingrained in oneself is quite more difficult.

Moreover, the realization of the multiple Factors of the Self, makes me aware about what I have and what I can' have or what I can work for and how it affects me. The social aspects, environmental, political, hereditary, and volition all affect us somehow in various degrees in different aspects of our self.

All in all, Module 1 helped me develop my character, self, and distinguish it from my identity and realized more about my self and others.
