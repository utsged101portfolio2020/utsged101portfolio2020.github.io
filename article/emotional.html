The Emotional Self

My emotional self is rather detached to me somehow often times as i am a person that has lots of things to be done. I keep my self productive to distract myself from negative emotions. This is I guess my method for self‐preservation. Sometimes I even invalidate my feelings in order to carry on and I have been doing this for quite some time now.

This is probably due to me being accustomed to being disappointed in a lot of situations just like having to go away from my friends due to change of residency, school, quite several times in my childhood and learned to suck it all up and not to bother pondering on.

However, these all saved me from wasting time and doing productive things that matter instead. Like making robots, inventions, and researches. Moreover, my teammates even commend me for professionalism despite personal difference that may sometimes impede others from working together.

But even with that, I still do empathize with people I know when they are troubled and even if they are an they're a person I do not like. I may invalidate my emotions but often I try not to invalidate others' feelings. More so, in Twitter I learn a lot of things about people's interactions due to the social, emotional issues I have learned from that platform and the insights real people have. That site has helped me in my character development more than our religion-based Values Education lectures in school which are plainly common sense. Learning from real people's experience has helped me improve my connection with my emotions better as well as special people who made me learn to feel again.
